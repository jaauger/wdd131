System Cost: A typical residential solar system can range from $10,000–$25,000 depending on size, equipment, and location. Costs can be reduced through federal and state incentives.

Long-Term Savings: Many homeowners see a dramatic reduction in electricity bills and often reach a break-even point within 6–10 years.

Federal Tax Credit: Homeowners may qualify for a significant tax credit when installing solar (percentage varies by year).

State or Utility Incentives: Some states offer rebates, property tax reductions, or net metering benefits.
